01 — The Problem

Fragmented data, reactive decisions

Many SMEs and project-based businesses operate with financial data scattered across ERP systems, contractor invoices, and payment records. Without integration, decision-making becomes entirely reactive.

  • Limited visibility into financial discrepancies across records
  • Delayed detection of invoice mismatches between ERP and contractors
  • Poor tracking of payment timelines and outstanding balances
  • High exposure to supplier compliance risks (BEE, certifications)
  • No structured environment for proactive governance
02 — The Solution

An integrated, centralized analytics platform

  • End-to-end reconciliation of ERP invoices, contractor invoices, and payments
  • Real-time identification of financial variances and mismatches
  • Monitoring of payment timelines and outstanding balances
  • Evaluation of supplier compliance — BEE status, certification validity, ownership thresholds
  • Risk classification across transactions and suppliers

The result is a centralized platform that supports both financial control and strategic decision-making — giving operational teams and executives a single source of truth.

03 — Architecture

Layered data pipeline

Built using a four-layer architecture to ensure scalability, traceability, and data integrity from raw input to executive dashboard.

①
Source Layer
Excel
Raw datasets: ERP invoices, contractor invoices, payments, suppliers, projects
②
Staging Layer
SQL
Flexible import structures preserving all records, including incomplete entries
③
Warehouse Layer
SQL
Cleaned, normalized fact & dimension tables with defined relationships
④
Analytics Layer
Power BI
Interactive dashboards delivering insights to operational and executive users
04 — Data Model (ERD)

Relational model ensuring integrity

Key relationships: Suppliers linked via supplier_id · Projects linked to ERP invoices · Payments linked through invoice_number · Reconciliation table as the central analytical layer.

05 — Dashboard Views

Five purpose-built reporting pages

Each page addresses a distinct business function — from high-level executive oversight to granular audit investigation.

Executive Overview

High-level KPIs and financial summary. Quick pulse-check for leadership on total spend, reconciliation status, and risk exposure.

Cash Flow & Payments

Payment trends over time, outstanding balances, and aging analysis. Identify bottlenecks in the payment cycle.

Audit Investigation

Drill-down into mismatches and anomalies between ERP and contractor records. Flags transactions requiring review.

Reconciliation Analysis

Deep-dive financial variance tracking. Side-by-side comparison of invoice sources with automated mismatch detection.

Supplier Compliance Monitor

BEE status, certification validity, ownership thresholds, and risk classification. Ensures procurement decisions are governance-ready and audit-proof.

06 — Key Insights

What the platform surfaces

Financial discrepancies between internal ERP records and external contractor invoices, identified automatically.

High-risk suppliers based on payment behaviour, compliance status, and BEE threshold violations.

Payment cycle inefficiencies — late payments, ageing balances, and cash flow pinch points.

Non-compliant suppliers with expired certifications or ownership thresholds below required levels.

Audit flags — transactions requiring investigation automatically surfaced before external review.

Governance improvement — stronger financial controls and reduced exposure to regulatory risk.
